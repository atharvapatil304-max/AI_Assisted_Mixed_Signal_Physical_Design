# RTL-to-GDS Flow Investigation

## Objective

One of the main objectives of Week 3 was to verify whether the `AMUX2_3V` analog hard macro is retained throughout the complete mixed-signal RTL-to-GDS implementation flow. During the Week 2 investigation, the absence of a standalone `AMUX2_3V.gds` file and the difficulty in locating the macro within the final layout created uncertainty about whether the macro had been removed during implementation. To resolve this question, the design was examined at every major stage of the physical design flow, beginning from the RTL source and ending with the generated GDS layout.

---

# Investigation Flow

The verification followed the implementation flow shown below.

```text
RTL
    ↓
Yosys Synthesis
    ↓
OpenROAD Floorplan
    ↓
Global Placement
    ↓
Detailed Routing
    ↓
Final DEF
    ↓
Generated GDS
```

At each stage, the generated netlist or physical database was searched for the `AMUX2_3V` instance to verify that the macro continued to exist throughout the implementation process.

---

The original RTL source (`design_mux.v`) was inspected to confirm that the analog macro was instantiated in the design.

# GDS Inspection

### Observation

Signal labels such as `select` and `out` were also visible, and the layout geometry corresponded closely with the placement information recorded in the DEF. These observations provide strong evidence that the analog macro is represented in the final physical layout.

# Key Findings

The investigation confirms that the `AMUX2_3V` analog hard macro is consistently preserved throughout the complete RTL-to-GDS implementation flow. The macro appears in every generated implementation netlist, is physically placed within the routed DEF database, and corresponds to a matching layout region in the final GDS. These observations collectively demonstrate that the analog macro successfully propagates through every major implementation stage without being removed.

---

# Conclusion

The complete stage-by-stage investigation verifies that the `AMUX2_3V` analog hard macro is retained throughout synthesis, floorplanning, placement, routing, DEF generation, and GDS stream-out. Although no standalone `AMUX2_3V.gds` file or `EXTRA_GDS_FILES` configuration was identified in the reproduced flow, the collected implementation evidence indicates that the macro is successfully represented in the final layout. This investigation resolves the uncertainty identified during Week 2 and confirms that the analog macro is maintained throughout the entire mixed-signal physical design flow.
